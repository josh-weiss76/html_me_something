The Journey Begins

My journey began on July 16, 1996 when my dad dropped me off at the recruiting station in Orlando, Florida. It was 6 in the morning and I was about to ship off for Basic Combat Training at Fort Leonard Wood, Missorri. This was the first step in my incredible journey, which has taken me around the country and the world.

Basic Combat Training

Upon arriving at the Chicago Airport, the other recruits and I were put on buses bound for Fort Leonard Wood and Basic Combat Training. This was also our first introduction to Drill Sergeants, and their special brand of motivational speaking.

The bus ride was a blur, it was the middle of the night and we were all nervous, anxious, and excited. We arrived at the Fort Leonard Wood recruit processing station at around 5 in the morning, and immediately began our second day in the Army. This included analytical tests, physical exams, immunizations, clothing issue, and a whole lot of waiting. The next several days were spent with the inprocessing unit, preparing for Basic Combat Training, and waiting for the next class to begin.

Basic Combat Training Graduation

When basic training began, the only thing that I expected was that it was going to be physically and mentally challenging. But after 8 long weeks of little sleep, long runs, ruck marches, haricuts, and daily physical smoke sessions, we graduated on September 19, 1996.

My dad drove up for the graduation, and I was able to spend some time with him the night before the graduation ceremony. We had a nice dinner on post and I was so happy to be able to spend that time with him. After the graduation ceremony, I was so excited to be done with basic training, that I completely forgot how to salute an Officer, and used my left hand. I immediately realized my mistake, and saluted with the correct hand. Dad drove me back home to Orlando, since I was able to take a week of leave before begining language training in California. I don't remember much about the ride home, we talked for a little bit, and I slept for the majority of the drive.

Cross Country Road Trip

When we got back to Florida, I spent several days hanging out with friends and family, telling stories about Basic Training and the friends that I had made there. My dad helped me plan my cross country drive from Orlando, Florida to the Presidio of Monterey, California. I left for my cross country trip in my 1994 Chevrolet Camaro early in the morning on Tuesday, September 24. I arrived at the Presidio of Monterey on the morning of Friday, September 27.

The Defense Language Institute, Foreign Language Center

Over the course of the next 12 months, I learned the Russian language and about its culture and people at the Defense Language Institue, Foreign Language Center . The language training began with learning the basice Russian alphabet and grew from there. After a couple of weeks, I was able to speak and understand basic commands and sentences. My class started with 10 students, which over time dwindled to four. Some students from previous classes were added, they had to restart for various reasons, and the class graduated with six students.

I graduated fromt the Russian Basic Course as the Honor Graduate on October 9, 1997.

Advanced Individual Training

After graduating from the Defense Language Institute, Foreign Language Center, I drove to Goodfellow Air Force Base (GAFB), Texas to begin my Advanced Individual Training (AIT) as a Signals Intelligence Analyst. The course took almost six months to complete and was comprised of two phases, with the first phase taking place at GAFB. The second phase took place at Fort Huachuca, Arizona. After almost two years of training, I finally graduated from AIT and was considered a full-fledged Soldier.

The Next 22 Years

Over the course of the next 22 years, I was stationed at Fort Hood, Texas; Camp Humphreys, Korea; Fort Meade, Maryland; Fort Hamilton, New York; Fort Bliss, Texas, back to Fort Meade, Maryland, and finally Fort Gordon, Georgia. I have had the opportunity to see Russia, Ukraine, and Colombia as part of my official military duties. I rose to the rank of Master Sergeant, and will retire on July 31, 2020 with the official rank of First Sergeant (Retired).
